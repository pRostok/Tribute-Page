Golda Meir

First woman to become head of government in Israel.

Here's a time line of Golda Meir's life:

  • 1898 - Born in Ukraine, Kiev
  • 1917 - After attending high school, Meir went to the Teachers' Training College in Milwaukee
  • 1917 - Married Morris Myerson
  • 1921 - left for Palestine. This Middle Eastern region, which included the territory of modern-day Israel and the West Bank, was at that time under the administration of Great Britain and largely populated by Arabs.
  • 1928 - Was the secretary of the Working Women's Council in Palestine and served as its representative on the leadership of the Histadruth.
  • 1929 - She was elected a delegate to most meetings of the World Zionist Organization. This was the real beginning of her Zionist political activity.
  • 1940 - She was appointed head of the political department of the Histadruth.
  • 1946 - Sas appointed acting head of the Jewish Agency's political department.
  • 1948 - she visited the United States to organize an emergency fund campaign for Palestine, with very successful results.
  • 1956 - Replace Shertok-Sharett as foreign minister, among the most important government jobs in the nation.
  • 1969 - resigned as minister of foreign affairs. However, soon after, under pressure from her political party, she agreed to take over the leadership of Israel's Labor Party.
  • 1973 - she resigned as prime minister.
  • 1951 - dies at the age of 80.
